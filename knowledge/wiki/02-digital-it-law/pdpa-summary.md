# Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) Summary

This page summarizes the Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (2019) of Thailand, which sets the standard for data privacy and protection.

> [!important] Extraterritorial Effect
> The PDPA applies to data controllers outside Thailand if they offer goods/services to individuals in Thailand or monitor behavior occurring within Thailand (Section 5).

## Key Definitions
- **Personal Data:** Information relating to a living person that enables identification (direct or indirect).
- **Data Controller:** Person/entity with the power to **decide** on data collection and use.
- **Data Processor:** Person/entity who processes data **on behalf of** or according to instructions from the controller.

## Legal Bases for Processing
Processing personal data without consent is only permitted under specific "legal bases" (Section 24):
1. **Contract:** Necessary for fulfilling a contract with the data subject.
2. **Legitimate Interest:** Necessary for the controller's or a third party's interests (unless overridden by the subject's rights).
3. **Legal Obligation:** To comply with other laws.
4. **Public Task:** For public interest or state authority.
5. **Vital Interest:** To prevent danger to life or health.
6. **Consent:** If no other basis applies, explicit consent must be obtained.

> [!danger] Sensitive Personal Data
> Data regarding race, political opinions, cults, religion, sexual behavior, criminal records, health, disability, labor union membership, genetics, or biometrics requires **Explicit Consent** or very specific legal exemptions (Section 26).

## Data Subject Rights
- **Access:** Request a copy of their data.
- **Portability:** Transfer data to another controller (only for Consent/Contract bases).
- **Objection:** Object to processing (e.g., for direct marketing).
- **Erasure (Right to be Forgotten):** Request deletion when data is no longer necessary.
- **Suspension:** Temporary stop on data usage during verification.
- **Rectification:** Keep data accurate and up-to-date.

## Compliance and Penalties
- **Data Breach Notification:** Must notify the PDPC within **72 hours** of discovery. If high risk, must also notify the data subject.
- **Penalties:**
    - **Civil:** Actual damages + Punitive damages (up to 2x).
    - **Criminal:** Up to 1 year imprisonment or 1 million THB fine.
    - **Administrative:** Fines up to 5 million THB.
