# Computer Crime Act Summary

The **Computer Crime Act B.E. 2550 (2007)**, and its 2017 amendment, governs activities in cyberspace in Thailand, defining offenses and the powers of officials to investigate and suppress digital crimes.

> [!info] Regulator
> The Minister of Digital Economy and Society (MDES) is the primary regulator.

## Common Offenses & Penalties

### Unauthorized Access
- **Systems (Section 5):** Accessing a system with specific security measures. (Up to 6 months / 10,000 THB).
- **Data (Section 7):** Accessing data with specific security measures. (Up to 2 years / 40,000 THB).

### Data Tampering & Disruption
- **Damage/Modification (Section 9):** Damaging, deleting, or altering someone else's computer data. (Up to 5 years / 100,000 THB).
- **System Interference (Section 10):** Disrupting a computer system so it cannot function normally. (Up to 5 years / 100,000 THB).

### Illegal Content (Section 14)
- **False Information:** Uploading false data that affects the public (e.g., fake news) or national security.
- **Obscene Material:** Uploading pornographic content accessible to the public.
- **Spreading:** Forwarding illegal content while knowing it is illegal.

> [!warning] Aggravated Circumstances
> If an offense affects national security, public safety, or causes death (even without intent to kill), penalties are significantly increased (up to 20 years or life imprisonment).

## Protections
- **Spam Protection (Section 11):** Sending unsolicited emails/messages (Spam) without an easy way to unsubscribe is subject to fines.
- **Fair Criticism (Section 16):** Editing photos of others is generally an offense if it causes damage, but "fair criticism" is an exception.
- **Safe Harbor (Section 15):** Service providers (ISPs/Platforms) who follow "Notice and Takedown" procedures may be exempt from liability for user-generated content.
