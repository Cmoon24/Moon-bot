# PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act)

The **Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (2019)** is Thailand's primary legislation governing the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data. It is heavily influenced by the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

## Core Roles

### Data Subject (เจ้าของข้อมูลส่วนบุคคล)
The individual to whom the personal data refers. The PDPA is designed to protect their rights and privacy.

### Data Controller (ผู้ควบคุมข้อมูลส่วนบุคคล)
The natural or juristic person having the power and duty to make decisions regarding the collection, use, or disclosure of personal data. They bear the primary responsibility for compliance.

### Data Processor (ผู้ประมวลผลข้อมูลส่วนบุคคล)
The natural or juristic person who operates in relation to the collection, use, or disclosure of the personal data pursuant to the orders given by or on behalf of a Data Controller.

## Fundamental Principles

### Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
Data must be processed based on a valid [[legal-bases-pdpa|Legal Basis]] and the subject must be informed of the purpose.

### Purpose Limitation
Data collected for one purpose cannot be used for another without new consent or a legal exemption.

### Data Minimization
Only the data necessary for the stated purpose should be collected.

## Sensitive Data
Certain categories of data are considered "sensitive" (e.g., health, criminal records, biometrics) and have stricter requirements for processing, usually requiring explicit consent.

## Enforcement Bodies
- **PDPC (Personal Data Protection Committee):** Sets policies and regulations.
- **Office of the PDPC:** Administrative and academic arm.
- **Expert Committee:** Handles complaints and issues administrative orders.
