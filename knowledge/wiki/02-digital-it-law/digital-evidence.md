# Digital Evidence

Digital evidence refers to any information stored or transmitted in digital form that can be used in legal proceedings. Its handling is regulated by the [[computer-crime-act-summary]].

## Preservation of Evidence
Victims of digital crime should:
- Capture screenshots.
- Record URLs and timestamps.
- Identify the perpetrator's identity where possible.

## Traffic Data (ข้อมูลจราจร)
Under Section 26, service providers must:
- Store traffic data (source, destination, route, time, volume) for at least **90 days**.
- In special cases, officials can order storage for up to **2 years**.

## Admissibility
Digital evidence is generally admissible in court, provided its integrity (authenticity and completeness) can be demonstrated. Under the [[electronic-transactions-act-summary]], electronic data is given the same legal weight as paper documents if stored and retrieved reliably.
