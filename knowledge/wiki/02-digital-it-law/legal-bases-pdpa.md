# Legal Bases for Processing (PDPA)

Under the Thailand [[pdpa|PDPA]], processing personal data is only legal if it falls under one of these specific bases (primarily Sections 24 and 26).

## Standard Bases (Section 24)
1. **Consent:** The data subject has given clear, voluntary permission.
2. **Contract:** Processing is necessary for a contract where the data subject is a party.
3. **Legal Obligation:** Necessary for the controller to comply with the law.
4. **Legitimate Interest:** Necessary for the interests of the controller or others, balanced against the subject's rights.
5. **Public Task:** Necessary for public interest or exercising state authority.
6. **Vital Interest:** To prevent danger to a person's life, body, or health.
7. **Research/Statistics:** For historical, research, or statistical purposes with appropriate safeguards.

## Sensitive Data Bases (Section 26)
Processing sensitive data (e.g., biometrics, health) usually requires **Explicit Consent** unless specific exemptions apply, such as:
- Prevention of life-threatening danger where consent cannot be given.
- Legitimate activities of non-profit bodies (e.g., unions, religious groups).
- Public interest in public health or social protection.
- Legal claims or court proceedings.
