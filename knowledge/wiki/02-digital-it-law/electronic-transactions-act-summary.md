# Electronic Transactions Act Summary

The **Electronic Transactions Act B.E. 2544 (2001)** establishes the legal framework to ensure that electronic data and signatures are recognized as equivalent to their paper counterparts in Thailand.

> [!info] Goal
> To create uniformity and align with international standards, enhancing trust in digital commerce.

## Legal Recognition (Section 7-13)
- **Legal Validity (Section 7):** No legal effect shall be denied to a message solely because it is in electronic form.
- **Writing Requirement (Section 8):** Electronic data satisfies the requirement for a document to be in writing if it is accessible and reusable without change.
- **Originality (Section 10):** Electronic data is considered an "original" if there is a reliable assurance of its integrity from the time it was first generated.
- **Admissibility (Section 11):** Courts cannot reject electronic data as evidence simply because it is digital.
- **Contractual Binding (Section 13):** Contracts can be formed via electronic "offers" and "acceptances".

## Digital Signatures
- **Recognition (Section 9):** An electronic signature is valid if it uses a method that can identify the signer and indicate their approval of the content.
- **Reliability (Section 26):** Defines what constitutes a "reliable" signature (e.g., data is uniquely linked to the signer and under their sole control).

> [!caution] Regulatory Compliance
> - **Notification Businesses:** Failure to notify or register specific digital services can result in up to 1 year in prison (Section 44).
> - **Licensed Businesses:** Operating without a required license can result in up to 2 years in prison (Section 45).
> - **Administrative Fines:** Can reach up to 2,000,000 THB for non-compliance.
