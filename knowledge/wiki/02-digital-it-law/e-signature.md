# Electronic Signature (ลายมือชื่ออิเล็กทรอนิกส์)

An electronic signature is any electronic sound, symbol, or process attached to or logically associated with a record and executed or adopted by a person with the intent to sign the record.

## Core Functions
According to the [[electronic-transactions-act-summary]], a signature must:
1. **Identify the Signer:** Reveal the identity of the person who created the signature.
2. **Indicate Intent:** Show that the person approves the information in the electronic data.

## Reliability Requirements
For a signature to be considered "reliable" (Section 26), it must:
- Be uniquely linked to the signer.
- Be created using data under the signer's sole control.
- Allow for the detection of any subsequent alteration to the signature or the data.

## Duties of the Signer
- Exercise reasonable care to prevent unauthorized use of their signature-creation data.
- Promptly notify relevant parties if the signature-creation data is compromised.
