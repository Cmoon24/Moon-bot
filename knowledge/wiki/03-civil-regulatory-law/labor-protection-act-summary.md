# Labor Protection Act Summary

The **Labor Protection Act B.E. 2541 (1998)**, and its 2019 amendment, sets the minimum standards for employment and labor use in Thailand to ensure fairness for both employers and employees.

> [!info] Scope
> Does NOT apply to central/regional/local government, or state-owned enterprises (Section 4).

## Key Employment Standards

### Working Hours & Holidays
- **Normal Hours:** Max 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week. Hazardous work is limited to 7 hours/day, 42 hours/week.
- **Rest Periods:** At least 1 hour/day after 5 consecutive working hours.
- **Weekly Holiday:** At least 1 day/week.
- **Traditional Holidays:** At least 13 days/year (including National Labor Day).
- **Annual Vacation:** At least 6 days/year after 1 year of service.

### Leave Entitlements
- **Sick Leave:** As long as actually sick. Paid up to 30 working days/year.
- **Maternity Leave:** Max 98 days (including check-ups). Paid up to 45 days.
- **Personal Business Leave:** At least 3 days/year (Paid).

## Prohibitions
- **Child Labor:** No hiring children under 15 (Section 44).
- **Sexual Harassment:** Explicitly prohibited (Section 16).
- **Pregnancy:** Cannot terminate employment due to pregnancy (Section 43).

> [!danger] Wage Deductions
> Deductions are generally prohibited except for specific legal requirements (taxes, union dues, coop debts, etc.) and must not exceed 10% per item (Section 76, 77).

## Mechanisms for Protection
- **Labor Inspectors:** Officials with power to inspect workplaces and handle employee complaints.
- **Severance Pay:** Mandatory compensation for termination without fault, based on length of service.
- **Punitive Fines:** If an employer willfully fails to pay on time, they may owe an additional 15% every 7 days (Section 9).
