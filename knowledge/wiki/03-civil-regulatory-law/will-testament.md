# Will (Testament)

A **Will** (พินัยกรรม) is a legal document that expresses a person's (the testator's) wishes as to how their property is to be distributed after their death.

## Validity Requirements
- **Age:** The testator must be at least 15 years old.
- **Mental Capacity:** Must be of sound mind at the time of making the will.
- **Form:** Must follow one of the forms prescribed by law (e.g., normal form, holographic, secret, oral).

## Common Forms in Thailand
1. **Normal Form:** Written, dated, and signed in front of two witnesses.
2. **Holographic Will:** Entirely handwritten, dated, and signed by the testator.
3. **Public Document Form:** Recorded at the District Office (Amphur) by a public official.

## Key Outcomes
- If a will is valid, its provisions take precedence over the statutory inheritance rules for the specific assets mentioned.
- If a legatee (beneficiary) dies before the testator, that specific provision in the will usually lapses unless stated otherwise.
