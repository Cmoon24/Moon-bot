# Debt Collection Act B.E. 2558 (2015) Summary

The **Debt Collection Act B.E. 2558** was enacted to protect debtors from inappropriate debt collection practices and to establish standards for debt collectors.

## Key Takeaways

> [!info] Contact Time Restrictions
> Debt collectors can only contact debtors during the following times:
> - **Mon-Fri**: 08:00 – 20:00
> - **Holidays**: 08:00 – 18:00
> *Source: Section 9(2)*

> [!warning] Privacy and Disclosure
> - It is prohibited to contact anyone other than the debtor for debt collection purposes, except to ask for the debtor's location (Section 8).
> - Debt collectors must not disclose the debt status to third parties (Section 11).

> [!danger] Prohibited Behaviors
> Under Sections 11, 12, and 13, the following are strictly forbidden:
> - **Violence**: Use of force, threats, or insults.
> - **Deception**: Pretending to be a court official, government officer, or lawyer.
> - **Unfair Practices**: Charging fees exceeding legal limits or inducing the debtor to issue a bad check.

## Action Plan for Rights Violations

If rights are violated (Sections 23 and 30):
1. **Gather Evidence**: Record calls, take photos of messages/letters, and note the collector's name.
2. **File a Complaint**: Contact the **District Office** or **Police Station** in the area.
3. **Follow Up**: The complaint will be forwarded to the Provincial/Bangkok Debt Collection Supervision Committee.

## Penalties
- **Violence/Deception**: Up to 5 years imprisonment or 500,000 THB fine (Sections 39-42).
- **Insults/Disclosure/Wrong Time**: Up to 1 year imprisonment or 100,000 THB fine.
