# Consumer Rights

Under the **Consumer Protection Act B.E. 2522**, consumers are entitled to five fundamental rights to ensure fairness and safety in the marketplace.

## The 5 Fundamental Rights
1. **Right to Information:** The right to receive accurate and sufficient descriptions and quality information about goods or services.
2. **Right to Freedom of Choice:** The right to choose to purchase goods or services by one's own volition and without unfair persuasion.
3. **Right to Safety:** The right to be safe from hazards that may arise from the use of goods or services.
4. **Right to Fair Contract:** The right to receive fairness in contractual terms when entering into agreements with businesses.
5. **Right to Redress:** The right to have grievances considered and to receive compensation for damages caused by violations of the above rights.

## Protection Mechanisms
These rights are protected through various committees under the [[ocpb]], including:
- Committee on Advertising
- Committee on Labeling
- Committee on Safety
- Committee on Contracts
