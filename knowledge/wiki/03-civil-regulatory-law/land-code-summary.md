# Land Code Summary

The **Land Code B.E. 2497** (and amendments) governs land ownership, rights, and management in Thailand.

## Key Takeaways

> [!important] Mandatory Registration
> Any transfer of land ownership or possession rights (for land with title deeds or certificates of utilization) must be made in writing and registered with the competent official. Otherwise, it is legally void.
> *Source: Section 4 bis*

> [!warning] Risk of Abandonment
> If a landowner abandons land for a continuous period exceeding the legal limit, the state may revoke the rights:
> - **Land with Title Deed**: Abandonment for more than **10 years**.
> - **Land with Certificate of Utilization**: Abandonment for more than **5 years**.
> *Source: Section 6*

> [!info] Land Documents (Title Deeds)
> - **โฉนดที่ดิน (Title Deed)**: Full ownership rights.
> - **น.ส. 3 (Certificate of Utilization)**: Proof of utilization.
> - **ใบจอง (Pre-emption Certificate)**: Temporary possession right (transfer prohibited except by inheritance).

## Action Plan for Disputes
1. **Boundary Disputes (Section 69 bis)**: Apply for survey, notify neighbors, and if mediation fails, sue within 90 days.
2. **Objecting to Official Orders (Section 60)**: File a court case within 60 days of the order.
3. **Correcting Errors (Section 61)**: The Director-General can investigate and revoke illegal deeds.
