# Inheritance Law Summary (CCC Book 6)

This page provides a summary of the legal principles regarding inheritance according to the Thailand Civil and Commercial Code (CCC) Book 6.

> [!info] Core Principle
> Inheritance (the estate) is transferred to heirs immediately upon the death of the decedent (Section 1599). The estate includes assets, rights, duties, and liabilities, but heirs are not liable for debts exceeding the value of the inheritance received (Section 1601).

## Key Components

### 1. Types of Heirs
There are two categories of heirs in Thai law:
1. **Statutory Heirs (ทายาทโดยธรรม):** Entitled by law due to blood relation or marriage.
2. **Legatees (ผู้รับพินัยกรรม):** Entitled by the decedent's will.

### 2. Statutory Heir Classes
Statutory heirs are divided into six classes, generally following the "near relatives exclude remote relatives" rule:
1. Descendants (Children, grandchildren, etc.)
2. Parents
3. Brothers and sisters of full blood
4. Brothers and sisters of half blood
5. Grandparents
6. Uncles and aunts

> [!tip] Special Rule
> Classes 1 (Descendants) and 2 (Parents) do not exclude each other; they are considered of the same rank for inheritance distribution.

### 3. Spousal Rights
A legally registered spouse is always a statutory heir. Their share depends on which class of heirs they are inheriting with:
- With Class 1: Equal to a child's share.
- With Class 2 or 3: One-half (1/2) of the estate.
- With Class 4, 5, or 6: Two-thirds (2/3) of the estate.
- If no other statutory heirs exist: The entire estate.

### 4. Wills and Forfeiture
- **Wills:** Must follow specific legal forms (e.g., written, witnessed). A will is void if the maker is under 15 years old.
- **Disinheritance:** A decedent can explicitly exclude a statutory heir via will or written notice to officials.
- **Forfeiture:** Heirs can lose rights by law for misconduct (e.g., killing the decedent).
- **Renunciation:** Heirs can renounce inheritance in writing after the decedent's death.

### 5. Administration and Statute of Limitations
- **Estate Administrator:** Can be appointed by the court to manage and distribute the estate.
- **Statute of Limitations (Prescription):**
    - **1 Year:** From when the heir knew or should have known of the death and their rights.
    - **10 Year (Max):** From the date of death, regardless of knowledge.

> [!warning] Urgent Action
> Heirs should check and manage the estate promptly to avoid losing rights due to the strictly enforced statute of limitations.
