# Civil and Commercial Code: Loan and Guarantee Summary

This summary covers the legal principles for loan contracts and guarantees under the Thai Civil and Commercial Code (CCC).

## Key Takeaways: Loan (กู้ยืมเงิน)

> [!important] Loan Validity
> A loan contract is legally binding only upon the **delivery of the money** (Section 650).

> [!info] Evidence for Lawsuits
> Loans exceeding **2,000 THB** require written evidence signed by the borrower to be enforceable in court (Section 653).

> [!warning] Interest Rate Limit
> Interest rates must not exceed **15% per year**. Any agreement exceeding this limit is void regarding the interest (Section 654). Compound interest is generally prohibited except under specific conditions (Section 655).

## Key Takeaways: Guarantee (ค้ำประกัน)

> [!info] Guarantor Notification
> If the debtor defaults, the creditor must notify the guarantor in writing within **60 days**. Failure to do so releases the guarantor from liability for interest and penalties occurring after that 60-day period (Section 686).

> [!danger] Void Agreements for Personal Guarantors
> Agreements requiring a personal guarantor to be liable as a **joint debtor** (ลูกหนี้ร่วม) are **void** (Section 681/1).

## Guarantor Rights
- **Right to Recourse (Section 693)**: After paying the debt, the guarantor can sue the debtor for the amount paid plus interest.
- **Right of Refusal (Sections 688-690)**: The guarantor can request the creditor to demand payment from the debtor or their assets first.
