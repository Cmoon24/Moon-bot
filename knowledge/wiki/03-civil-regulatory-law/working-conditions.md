# Working Conditions

Working conditions in Thailand are strictly regulated by the [[labor-protection-act-summary]] to ensure the health, safety, and well-being of employees.

## Standards
- **Maximum Working Hours:** Generally 8 hours per day and 48 hours per week.
- **Mandatory Rest:** At least one hour of rest after five consecutive hours of work.
- **Overtime (OT):** Must be voluntary (in most cases) and paid at higher rates (1.5x to 3x).
- **Holidays:** Minimum of one weekly holiday, 13 traditional holidays per year, and 6 annual vacation days after one year of work.

## Safety and Health
Employers are responsible for providing a safe work environment, including:
- Protective equipment for hazardous work.
- Restrictions on heavy lifting (especially for women and children).
- Prohibitions on working in dangerous locations (e.g., mines, scaffolding) for certain protected groups.
