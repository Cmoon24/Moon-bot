# Severance Pay (ค่าชดเชย)

Severance pay is the mandatory compensation an employer must pay to an employee when they are terminated without fault (Section 118 of the Labor Protection Act).

## Rates Based on Service Duration
| Duration of Service | Severance Pay Rate |
| :--- | :--- |
| 120 days - 1 year | 30 days of wages |
| 1 year - 3 years | 90 days of wages |
| 3 years - 6 years | 180 days of wages |
| 6 years - 10 years | 240 days of wages |
| 10 years - 20 years | 300 days of wages |
| 20 years or more | 400 days of wages |

## Key Rules
- **Non-Fault Termination:** Only applicable if the employee is not terminated for cause (e.g., serious misconduct, theft).
- **Retirement:** Considered a form of termination that entitles the employee to severance pay (Section 118/1).
- **Interest and Penalties:** Failure to pay entitles the employee to 15% annual interest and potential punitive "extra money" of 15% every 7 days if the delay is willful.
