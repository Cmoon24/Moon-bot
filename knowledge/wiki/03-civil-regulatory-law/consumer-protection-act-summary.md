# Consumer Protection Act Summary

This page summarizes the Consumer Protection Act B.E. 2522 (1979) of Thailand, which establishes the fundamental rights of consumers and the mechanisms for their protection.

> [!info] Broad Definition
> A **Consumer** includes buyers, service recipients, and those "invited or offered" by businesses to buy goods or services. Protection starts even before a transaction occurs (Section 3).

## 5 Basic Consumer Rights
According to Section 4, consumers have the right to:
1. **Information:** Receive accurate and sufficient descriptions of goods/services.
2. **Freedom of Choice:** Choose goods or services without coercion.
3. **Safety:** Be safe from hazards arising from the use of goods/services.
4. **Fair Contract:** Receive fairness in contractual terms (added in 1998).
5. **Redress:** Have their grievances considered and receive compensation for damages.

## Regulated Areas
The Act focuses on four main pillars of business conduct:
- **Advertising:** Prohibits false, exaggerated, or misleading statements.
- **Labeling:** Requires accurate labels (price, origin, expiry) for controlled goods.
- **Safety:** Imposes proactive duties on businesses to monitor and track safe products.
- **Contracts:** Prohibits unfair terms in "contract-controlled" businesses.

> [!warning] Supplementary Law
> This Act acts as a "supplement" to other specific laws. It applies if a specific law is silent or provides less protection than this Act (Section 21).

## Mechanisms for Redress
- **OCPB (สคบ.):** The Office of the Consumer Protection Board handles complaints and can sue businesses on behalf of consumers.
- **Free Litigation:** When the OCPB or its officials sue on behalf of a consumer, they are **exempt from all court fees** (Section 39).
- **Direct Orders:** Specific committees (Advertising, Safety, Labeling, Contract) can issue direct orders to businesses to stop or correct violations.
