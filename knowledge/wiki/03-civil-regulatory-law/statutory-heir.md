# Statutory Heir

A **Statutory Heir** (ทายาทโดยธรรม) is a person entitled by law to receive a portion of a decedent's estate due to their specific relationship with the decedent (blood or marriage).

## Classes of Statutory Heirs
The Civil and Commercial Code identifies six classes:
1. **Descendants:** Children (including legal children of the spouse and adopted children).
2. **Parents:** Biological parents.
3. **Brothers and Sisters of Full Blood.**
4. **Brothers and Sisters of Half Blood.**
5. **Grandparents.**
6. **Uncles and Aunts.**

## Distribution Rules
- **Near relative excludes remote:** If there are heirs in class 1, they exclude classes 3-6.
- **Exception for Class 2:** Parents (Class 2) inherit alongside Descendants (Class 1) and are considered of equal rank.
- **The Spouse:** A legal spouse is always a statutory heir and inherits a specific portion alongside the classes mentioned above.
