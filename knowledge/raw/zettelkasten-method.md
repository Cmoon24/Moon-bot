# The Zettelkasten Method

The Zettelkasten (German for "slip box") is a system of note-taking and personal knowledge management used in research and study. It was popularized by the sociologist Niklas Luhmann, who credited it for his enormous productivity (publishing over 70 books and 400 articles).

## Key Principles

1. **Atomicity**: Each note should contain exactly one idea.
2. **Autonomy**: Each note should be self-contained and understandable on its own.
3. **Connectivity**: Notes are linked to each other to form a web of ideas.
4. **No Hierarchy**: The system is organic and bottom-up rather than top-down.

## The Slip Box
The physical (or digital) box where the notes live. Each note has a unique identifier (address) that allows for branching and linking.
